feat(ties): add Irving's polynomial super-stable matching algorithm

Add `super_stable_irving`, the O((P*R)^2) counterpart to the brute-force
`super_stable`. Free men propose to their entire most-preferred indifference
class; a woman deletes every man she strictly ranks below a current proposer;
a woman left engaged to two or more men breaks all engagements and deletes her
tail class. The settled engagement relation is the proposer-optimal
super-stable matching, or None when none exists. Reduces to Gale-Shapley on
strict lists.

Verified against the brute-force oracle on existence and validity over strict
(== Gale-Shapley, 500), complete tiered (3000), and individually-rational
incomplete tiered (3000) markets. The incomplete case uses an IR-enforcing
oracle, since the existing blocking-only `is_super_stable` (repo convention)
does not by itself reject matchings to unacceptable partners.

This is the super-stability mechanism behind Basu's (2025) Extended-Gale-
Shapley competing-bandits-in-matching-markets result, giving the ties module a
polynomial super-stable solver for that bridge.
